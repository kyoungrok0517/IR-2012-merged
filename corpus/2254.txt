He graduated summa cum laude in 1925 from Harvard with a degree in physics.

He got his Ph.D. from the University of Goettingen in 1926 at the age of 22.

In 1927 he returned to Harvard to study mathematical physics on a fellowship.

In 1928, he did post-grad studies at Cal Tech; he did not get a degree.

He then became a professor at both Cal Tech and UC-Berkeley.
