Go to this site right here: http://www.treasurydirect.gov/BC/SBCPrice


You'll need to know the series, denomination, issue date, and serial number. This can all be found on the face of each of the bonds. They're most likely not worth much yet, but if you're able to hold on to them for many more years they will eventually appreciate in value and you can then cash them in at any bank. If you don't feel like you can keep track of them for long enough for them to reach their full value, you can take them to the bank and cash them in today for whatever value they are worth.
